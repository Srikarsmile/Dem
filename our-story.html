Our Story

The journey that inspired Dignitate

Elizabeth Gill's Journey

1940

Birth & Early Life

Elizabeth was born on October 15, 1940 in Rawalpindi, Pakistan. Orphaned during the Partition of India, she was raised by extended family members who instilled in her the values of compassion and service.

1960s

Nursing Training

Elizabeth pursued her passion for helping others through nursing training at Holy Family Hospital in Rawalpindi. Her dedication to patient care earned her recognition and respect from colleagues and patients alike.

1960s

Military Service

She served as a military staff nurse, providing care to service members and their families. This experience deepened her commitment to serving those in need.

Late 1960s

Jordan

Elizabeth worked in Jordan, where she was inspired by Princess Muna's dedication to healthcare. This international experience broadened her perspective on nursing care.

1974

New Beginning in England

Elizabeth relocated to England and began working at Sidcup Hospital, bringing her years of experience and cultural perspective to British healthcare.

1975

Marriage

Elizabeth married Daniel Gill, beginning a new chapter in her life while continuing her nursing career.

1976-1980

Family

Malcolm was born in 1976, followed by his brother in 1980. Elizabeth balanced her roles as a dedicated mother and nurse, continuing to work at Mayday University Hospital.

1984

Work Accident

Elizabeth suffered a work accident that resulted in broken clavicles, the first of several health challenges she would face.

1985

Brain Haemorrhage

A brain haemorrhage left Elizabeth with left-side sensation loss, forcing her into early retirement from the nursing career she loved.

2011

Vascular Dementia Diagnosis

Elizabeth was diagnosed with Vascular Dementia. Malcolm became her primary caregiver for the next 11 years, navigating the challenges of dementia care within the South Asian community.

2022

Legacy

Elizabeth passed away in 2022, but her legacy lives on through Dignitate. Her life of service inspired Malcolm to create an organization that supports others facing similar challenges.

Why Dignitate Was Founded

During the 11 years Malcolm cared for his mother, he observed several challenges that many South Asian families face:

  • Reduced family visitation after dementia diagnosis due to cultural stigma and misunderstanding
  • Communication gaps among healthcare providers, especially during COVID-19
  • Lack of culturally sensitive resources and support for South Asian carers
  • Limited understanding of dementia as a medical condition rather than a mental health issue
