Our Story

A Personal Journey

After spending 11 years caring for my mother, who had vascular dementia, I felt compelled to start a charity to help others going through similar situations. Throughout my experience, I learned the ins and outs of being a caregiver and found that there were many challenges and difficulties that come with caring for a loved one with dementia.

However, I also learned the importance of support, community, and resources in making the journey more manageable. Through my charity, I hope to provide a platform for caregivers to share their experiences, connect with others in similar situations, and learn from my own experiences. I want to let others know that they are not alone and that there are people who care and understand what they are going through.

Mission

Dignitate empowers cultural carers and families, with dementia as our core focus, through community-led networks, culturally competent support, and preventative care. We amplify carers’ voices, dismantle barriers, and build stronger, more inclusive communities.

Vision

We envision a world where cultural carers are fully valued, supported, and recognised, with services and communities working together to ensure equity, dignity, and inclusion.

Understanding Early Signs of Dementia

Learn to recognize the early signs of dementia (known as 'Pagal' in some South Asian communities). Understand the difference between normal aging and dementia symptoms.
