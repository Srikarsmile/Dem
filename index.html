About Dignitate

Placing Dignity at the Heart of Dementia Care

Dignitate is a registered charity (1196886) founded by Malcolm Gill, who lost his mother to vascular dementia in 2022. As a British Asian, Malcolm was raised in a culture that often perceives dementia as a mental condition rather than a neurodegenerative disease.

Our mission is to place dementia and caring within cultural communities at the heart of health equity. We empower carers and families—often unseen and underserved—to access support, share experiences, and sustain their wellbeing alongside those they care for.

Courses designed by experts in the field, ensuring the highest quality education and support for you and your family.

Understanding Early Signs of Dementia

Learn to recognize the early signs of dementia (known as 'Pagal' in some South Asian communities). Understand the difference between normal aging and dementia symptoms.

  • Culturally sensitive approach
  • Practical tips for families
  • Expert-led sessions

Multi-Sensory Stimulation

Discover how to use multi-sensory stimulation techniques to improve quality of life and communication with those living with dementia.

Understanding and coping with anticipatory grief—the mourning that occurs before a death when watching a loved one's gradual decline.

Our Partnership

Working with NHS Our Future Health

We are proud to partner with NHS Our Future Health, the UK's largest health research programme. Together, we're working to improve healthcare for dementia patients and increase representation of South Asian communities in vital medical research.
